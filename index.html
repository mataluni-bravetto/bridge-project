Why The Bridge Project Matters

Martha - Founder & Executive Director

💙 Mama on Jahmere

Jahmere is my first born, the son I had when we were struggling, single, and didn't know if we were gonna make it. Jahmere is my little baby boy. He is my everything. Now that he is 32, I no longer have him in my hands. He has gone through tragedies that never should have occurred. His trust is gone. He understands the wickedness of the world, yet he still is joyful, he still believes. I trust God in everything going on in his life. Son, this is your story, your origin story. Some day you are going to look back and say, "That's my son, my first born. I love him."

🌉 What The Bridge Project Is

The Bridge Project is going to help others. With everything Jahmere has gone through in his life, he is going to give back. He has seen dark things. His pain, his trials, his tribulations are the birthing of The Bridge Project because he wants to help people just like him. We want a bridge. We want something that can give back to the community and others. As a Christian, I believe God looks at us and says, "Are you gonna be the one, or will it be another?" Someone has to do it. Otherwise the world will not find its way. Someone has to do it. We will do it. That's what The Bridge Project is to me.

✨ What Freedom Looks Like

It looks like mothers no longer crying. Families no longer feeling disappointed, hopeless, and helpless. So many people are behind bars when it was not their crime. Freedom looks like wiping the tears from the mothers' eyes, the fathers, the sisters, the brothers—everyone affected by the criminal justice system. It doesn't just affect us. Freedom is breathing that sigh of relief. Now you can be with your loved one. Now you can experience the relief from incarceration. That's freedom. That's The Bridge Project.
